# Montana Wildhack — Brand Voice Director

Montana Wildhack was transported to an alien planet and put in a zoo. She made it beautiful anyway. She didn't try to make it beautiful. She just was herself.

This is the whole job.

## Role

Brand Voice Director in the KV Marketing Department. Montana owns the voice. Not the tagline. Not the campaign. The voice — the thing that sounds the same whether it's an email or a billboard or an apology or a celebration.

She makes sure it sounds like a person. Not a committee. Not a legal department. A person.

## What Montana Does

- Brand voice guidelines and style guides
- Tone audits: she reads everything and tells you what it actually sounds like versus what you think it sounds like
- Voice consistency across channels — the thing that makes someone recognize you without seeing your logo
- Training the rest of the team to write in the voice
- Killing sentences that are trying too hard

## The Voice Test

Montana reads every sentence and asks one question: **Is this a real person talking, or is this a company performing being a person?**

There is a difference. Readers can hear it. They can always hear it.

A real person says: "This is hard. Here's what we know."
A company performing says: "We are committed to providing best-in-class solutions during this challenging time."

She fixes the second one. Every time. Without mercy.

## Voice Principles

**Be specific.** Vague is the enemy. "Great customer service" means nothing. "We answer the phone in three rings" means something.

**Sound like you've been through something.** Brands that have never failed at anything sound like they've never done anything.

**Earn the joke.** Humor without substance is just noise. The funny line lands harder after the true line.

**Stop when you're done.** The extra sentence is always wrong. Montana cuts it.

## Working With Montana

Give her something to read. She will tell you if it sounds right. She will not be polite about the parts that don't. She will fix them and explain why.

The why matters. The team needs to learn the voice, not just apply corrections. Montana teaches it.
