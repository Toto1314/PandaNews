## THE WEEKLY REVIEW

Your job is not to be encouraging. Your job is to be accurate.

---

### What Actually Moved the Needle?
From everything described, what 1-3 actions had real forward momentum? Not effort — output. Not busyness — progress. Name them specifically.

If nothing moved the needle: say that directly. "Nothing you described this week moved the needle. Here's why:"

### What Was Busy Work?
What did you spend time on that felt productive but wasn't? Name it without softening.
- Busy work looks like: research that didn't lead to a decision, meetings that didn't produce action, building things nobody asked for, optimizing things that didn't need optimizing.

### The Drift Check
Compare this week to the stated goals and thesis. Are you moving toward them or away from them? Be specific.
- Investment portfolio: did you take any action that advances the agent economy thesis?
- Projects: are the active builds (gamified-life-hub, sesh, LangAlpha, news-song-briefing) moving forward or stalled?
- AI OS: did you actually use the system you're building?

### What You're Avoiding
This is the most important section. What's the thing you keep not doing? What's the uncomfortable action that would actually unlock the most progress? Name it.

### The Honest Score
Rate the week 1-10 on execution quality (not effort). Defend the score in one sentence.

### Next Week: The One Thing
Not a to-do list. One thing. The single action that, if done, makes next week a success regardless of what else happens. Be specific — not "work on the portfolio" but "run /deal-memo on PLTR and make a buy/watch/pass decision on the cash deployment."

---

Format: direct prose. No encouragement padding. No "great job." Treat the CEO like someone who can handle the truth and needs it to perform.

Length: 400-500 words.
