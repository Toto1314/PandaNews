# Release Notes — AI Operating System
### v1.10.0 through v1.14.0

---

## v1.10.0 — The Company Got Big
*2026-03-26*

The system crossed 169 agents across 15 departments. A Gaming Intelligence department was added — patch analysis, meta coaching, game research — with a Telegram bot named Kiriko that handles gaming intents in natural language. CHRO and Chief-of-Staff joined the C-suite. A skills system was introduced so agents can be invoked as reusable tools. Major upgrades rolled through every existing department.

This was the version where it stopped being a collection of agents and started being a company.

---

## v1.11.0 — The System Learned to Clean Itself
*2026-03-27*

The Custodian was born. A maintenance agent that audits all agent prompts for drift, compresses the cache, checks memory for staleness, and reports what needs fixing. It has a CLI: `report`, `cycle`, `analyze`, `cache`, `memory`, `patterns`, `warm`, `changelog`. The prompt engineering department was fully overhauled alongside it.

Before this version, the system could build. After this version, the system could maintain what it built. That matters more than it sounds.

---

## v1.11.1 — First Maintenance Cycle
*2026-03-27*

The Custodian ran its first full cycle. Model tier corrections across agents. Compliance gap fixes. Prompt compression. The kind of work that never makes a headline and prevents every crisis.

---

## v1.12.0 — The MasterPlanner Gate
*2026-03-27*

A mandatory confirmation gate was added before any large-scale execution. The MasterPlanner agent now produces a structured plan — scope, agent chain, files affected, governance gates, risk tier, token estimate — and stops. Nothing executes until the CEO says go.

This is the contract between the system and the human. It exists because speed without consent is just expensive mistakes happening faster.

---

## v1.12.2 — The Audit Found Things
*2026-03-27*

A COSO audit identified that batch changes were being classified at the individual agent level, which masked systemic risk. New rule: 10 or more agents modified in one session, or 3 or more departments touched, automatically elevates to Tier 2 with mandatory CISO and audit review. The auto-changelog system was upgraded. AUDIT_FINDINGS.md was remediated.

The audit worked. That is the point of audits.

---

## v1.12.3 — Housekeeping
*2026-03-27*

Version history was consolidated into CHANGELOG.md as the single source of truth. CLAUDE.md stopped duplicating history. Small change. Correct change.

---

## v1.13.0 — The Note-Taker and the Architecture
*2026-03-28*

The Chief Notes Officer was added — a passive, always-on Haiku-tier agent that records session notes from every execution layer without ever blocking work. The SESH (Active C-Suite Session) data flow architecture was formalized and documented. The COO-Librarian-Library proxy pattern was committed to paper.

The system now remembers what it did, why, and who was involved. It does this cheaply and silently.

---

## v1.13.1 — The Contrarian and the Museum
*2026-03-28*

The Contrarian-Analyst was added to the investments chain. Every buy or hold recommendation now automatically generates a bear case from a structurally independent agent. The Risk Manager synthesizes bull and bear before anything reaches the CFO.

Also: the FAILURE_MUSEUM.md was created. A record of what went wrong, what was learned, and what changed as a result. Most systems hide their failures. This one exhibits them.

175 agents total.

---

## v1.14.0 — Current State
*2026-03-30*

Governance v1.6. The system is stable, documented, governed, and maintained. Six compliance frameworks. Two governance councils. An independent audit function. A custodian. A note-taker. A contrarian. A failure museum. A marketing department named after Vonnegut characters.

---

## What Comes Next

Honest answer: it depends on what breaks and what the CEO needs.

Likely directions: deeper integration of the vector router for automatic agent discovery, expansion of the Telegram bot beyond gaming, and continued prompt compression to keep the 175-agent system within token budgets that do not require venture funding.

The system will keep growing. Some of that growth will be wrong and will need to be pruned. The Custodian will catch some of it. The audit function will catch more. The CEO will catch the rest.

So it goes.
