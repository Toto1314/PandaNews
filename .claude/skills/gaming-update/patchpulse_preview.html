Valorant
Patch 12.05
Updated today · 2026-03-25 · Act 2 begins

Act 2 launches with new Controller Miks reshaping the support meta, while Yoru and Clove — the two most dominant flex picks of Act 1 — both get gutted simultaneously. The duelist meta just got a hard reset and Controller players are eating well.

Buffed
Skye — Guiding Light (Flashes)
Flashes are now rechargeable on a 60-second cooldown instead of consuming limited charges. Initiator players who stay alive get rewarded with a second or third flash mid-round.
Play this now
Miks (New Agent — Controller)
Sound-based kit: dual-use grenade (heals allies or concusses enemies), smokes, Combat Stim that refreshes on kills shared with a teammate, and an ultimate that knocks back + deafens + slows an entire push.
Worth trying
Lotus (Map Returns)
Attacker-side wall pen removed, jump to Vines repositioned, Tree Room wall reinforced. Attacking A-site is less miserable. Wall-bang cheese is gone.
Situational
Nerfed
Yoru
Gatecrash beacon duration cut from 30s → 15s. Blindside charges reduced from 2 → 1. One flash means one real play per round — no more double-flash fakeout entries.
Still playable
Clove
Ruse post-death duration: 14s → 6s. Meddle AoE: 6m → 4m. 6 seconds of smoke after death barely buys a push. The entire design proposition is significantly weaker.
Shelve it
OP Right Now
Miks + Skye / KAY/O
Stim-share into flash-clear is the new fastest entry package. This combo will get solved in pro play first but it's already broken in ranked.
Omen / Viper
With Clove nerfed, both dominant solo-Controller picks fill the vacuum. Omen's one-way smokes on the reworked Lotus A-site are genuinely oppressive.
Dead
Clove below Diamond
The post-death window is too short for most players to execute on. Drop rate in ranked will be steep.
Yoru soloqueue
Two charges of Blindside covered for bad coordination. One charge does not. Off the ranked table for most players.
Abyss & Corrode
Both maps removed from competitive rotation.
Sleeper Pick
Skye's recharge system
A Skye who plays deep and gets value can cycle back into a second or third flash within the same round. Most opponents won't adjust to this for at least two weeks — it's a psychological edge that the scoreboard won't show.
Meta Verdict
Patch 12.05 is a genuine shake-up. The two safest picks from Act 1 both got hit hard in the same patch, Lotus is back with attacker-favored fixes, and a new high-ceiling Controller just entered the pool. This week belongs to Controller players — Omen and Viper move from "solid" to "dominant by default" while Miks gets figured out. On the Duelist side, Jett and Reyna are your best bets. Play Controller, learn Lotus A-site fast, and if you're running Initiator, lock Skye and do not waste flashes early.
